Amature cyclist

Story

I grew up racing around on bicycles with my neighborhood friends.In my 1st year, my sister bought a gearless bicycle and wasn’t using it, so I began riding it for fun. Later I read about @ganeshabybike And IPS officer Annamalai , who used to promote cycling culture.They both inspired me. I was introduced to Udupi Cycling Club (UCC) by a friend. I started to riding UCC and Ganesh nayak in his morning 6am rides from Tiger Circle.

After that I made a big move to buy a geared cycle (21 speed). Rides were usually solo, but joined a lot of rides with friends, UCC rides, Ganesh sir’s rides and few seniors (because of whom I started improving).

In 2nd year, I went to Mangalore for a race with a borrowed cycle and almost won. After couple of months i got in touch with @godfrey_pinto, a Mangalorean who stays in UAE in a race in held in Mangalore, motivated me to race more. I began training under him. I trained hard, and attended couple of races in Mangalore and got a taste of racing (MACC, WERC races).

It was in 3rd year when I finally decided to get a proper Roadbike,@regan_loy from st. antonys cycle helped me in getting a good discount from Track and trail. Trough lot of solo training and some group with my riding partners I started getting stronger. I attempted many races in Bangalore and Mangalore. Later, I began training under Godfrey’s mentor, Ronnie George who resides in Dubai. Racing with people like Shashank pal, Dhanraj Karkera, and many other pro riders encouraged me even more to get serious in racing. @naweenwithav was also one of my key inspirations.

So far I have completed a several brevets (200km/300km) and cycled around 30,000+ km of distance in past 4 years. Till date, I have participated in around 32 races across Mangalore, Bangalore, Kerala, Chennai, Mysore, Udupi etc. and have got podium finishes in 13 races, and looking forward to improving my ranking at States and one day, competing in Nationals.
